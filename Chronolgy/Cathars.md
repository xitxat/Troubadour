# Cathar Chronology (Condensed from cathar.info)

## 100 BC – AD 100
- Gnostic movements flourish in the eastern Mediterranean.

## 216–277
- 216 – Birth of Manes in Mesopotamia; later executed for founding a dualist religion.

## Early 11th Century
- Theodora, Empress of Byzantium, has many Paulicians executed.
- 1012 – First known Cathars recorded in the Limousin.
- 1022 – Dualists burned at Orléans; similar executions at Toulouse.
- 1028 – Council of Charroux seeks ways to combat “error” (early anti-Cathar measures).

## Mid 11th – 12th Century
- 1045–1056 – Bishops report spread of the sect; councils (Reims, Toulouse) threaten or condemn “heretics”.
- 1118 – Bogomils executed by Emperor Alexius Comnenus.
- 1119 – Council of Toulouse charges secular powers with harsh action against heresy.
- 1135–1148 – Councils (Pisa, Reims) refine church procedures and penalties against heretics.
- 1163 – Council of Tours defines procedures to investigate and discipline suspected heretics.
- 1165 – Council at Lombez condemns the boni homines (“good men”).
- 1167 – Cathar Council at Saint-Félix de Caraman; sets up Cathar organisation in Languedoc.
- 1179 – Third Lateran Council anathematises the Cathar “heresy”.

## Late 12th Century
- 1180–1181 – Armed expedition against Lavaur, a “heretic” stronghold.
- 1183–1206 – Neo-Manichaeans persecuted in Auxerre (confiscations, exile, burnings).
- 1195 – Council of Montpellier.
- 1197–1198 – Peter II of Aragon orders expulsion of Waldensians and other schismatics; Council of Gerona publishes the edict.
- 1198 – Pontificate of Pope Innocent III begins (ends 1216).

## 1200–1206
- 1200–1204 – Multiple burnings of Cathars in Troyes, Nevers, Braisne-sur-Vesle, and Paris.
- 1204 – Philip Augustus orders further burnings; Peter II of Aragon convenes debates between Catholics, Cathars, and Waldensians at Carcassonne.
- 1204 – Cathar Council of Mirepoix; Raymond de Pereille tasked with rebuilding Montségur as a Cathar stronghold.
- 1205 – Papal bull forbids legal help for heretics.
- 1206 – Dominic Guzmán preaches in Languedoc; largely fails to convert Cathars.
- 1206 – Esclarmonde of Foix receives the consolamentum and becomes a Parfaite.
- 1206 – Foundation of Prouille, a Dominican convent intended as a counter to Cathar houses.

## 1207–1209 (Immediate Prelude to Crusade)
- 1207 – Colloquy of Montréal / Pamiers: debates between Catholics, Cathars, and Waldensians.
- 1208-01-15 – Murder of Pierre (Peter) de Castelnau near Saint-Gilles after a failed meeting with Raymond VI.
- 1208–1209 – Innocent III escalates towards full crusade; Council of Avignon (1209) excommunicates Raymond VI.

## Key Later Milestones (Headlines Only)
- 1209–1229 – Major battles, sieges, and councils of the Albigensian Crusade (Béziers, Carcassonne, Lavaur, Toulouse, Muret, Beaucaire, Marmande, Avignon, Montségur, etc.).
- 1220s–1250s – Second wave of crusading, consolidation of Capetian control, and growth of inquisitorial structures.
- 1271 – Annexation of Languedoc into the French crown domains.
- 1321 – Execution of Guillem Bélibaste, often cited as the last known Cathar Perfect.
